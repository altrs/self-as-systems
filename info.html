DATA SELF PORTRAIT

Research question: On an individual level and collective level, what defines a solitary experience?

This webpage is meant to be an exploration of systems of thought. Based on the user's answers on the questionaire ,the webpage 'environment' will ultimately reflect the user's outlook of what it means to 'be alone'

System of thought #1 :
No one is around me, I am alone. There's no person/thing around me that I am perceiving right now as cognizant, or want to interact/engage with in the present moment
System of thought #2 :
No one is around me, but I am not alone. While there are no people around you, there are many entities you can choose to interact or engage with, if you want to.

While both systems of thought are valid, the first system of thought focuses on acknowleding actuality through 2-way communication. (Humans talking to humans; human petting a dog; dog barking at other dog).

The second system of thought is 'proactive' : going out of your own way to acknowledge another's actuality whether or not there was 2-way communication (i.e. talking to a plant). This system of thought requires you to disregard boundaries and labels. Disregard species difference, location difference, time difference, or even sentience difference.

***

It's easy to say this is a silly idea; however, maybe it's a mental excersize worth trying. What does this new thought system ultimately do? :

1. Responsibility (See link to excerpt from Laura McBride below)

2. Choice: You are not completely dependent on others to tell you what is or what isn't. Leading to active mental engagement with one's surroundings, curiosity, creative thinking, critical thinking, tapping into inner-child openmindedness.

3. Compassion for your environment
